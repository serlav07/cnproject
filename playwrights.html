Playwrights

2000 - 2020


Ella Hickson is an award-winning writer whose work has been performed throughout the UK and abroad. Her most recent play Oil, opened at the Almedia Theatre in October 2017. In 2011, Ella was the Pearson Writer-in-Residence at the Lyric Theatre Hammersmith ans she was the recipient of Catherine Johnson Award.
The Writer tells the story of a woman who is trying to de-construct the patriachial world she lives in. She is a writer and she believes stories don't neccessarily need to have a beginning, a middle and an end.

Jackie Kay has written extensively for stage and television. Her plays, Manchester Lines (produced by Mancester Library Theatre) and The New Maw Broon Monologues (produced by Glasgay) were a great success. She was awarded an MBE in 2006 and made fellow of the Royal Society of Literature in 2002.
Chiaroscuro tells the story of three women, all mixed raced, that are united through friendship but seemed to be divided by the way they were brought up. It's a story about a beautiful friendship, identity and belonging.

Sabrina Mahfouz is the editor of The Things I Would Tell You: British Muslim Women Write , a 2017 Guardian Book of the Year. She has recently been elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of Literature and is the recipient of the 2018 King's Alumni Arts and Culture Award.
A History of Water in the Middle East is a piece of gig theatre mixed with a history lesson on collonialism and the expansion of the British Empire in the Middle East. It's also a heart wrenching story about people, people who have died and are dying as victims of the power war between the world's most powerful countries.
